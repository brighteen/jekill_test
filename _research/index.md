I am an undergraduate researcher focusing on Machine Learning, Generative Models, Representation Learning, and Physical AI. Rather than utilizing state-of-the-art applications, I pursue the underlying mathematical rigor of linear algebra and optimization theory to understand generative architectures and connect them with physical environments. This foundational focus directly aligns with my current academic trajectory in theoretical optimization and physical world modeling.  

Recently, I have extended my research interests to Cognitive Science and Neuroscience. This expansion stems from a desire to bridge the fundamental gap between compute-intensive empirical AI and the highly sample-efficient learning mechanisms of human cognition and neural structures. Investigating these biological paradigms will ultimately serve as a core foundation for building efficient Physical AI systems that seamlessly interact with dynamic environments.
